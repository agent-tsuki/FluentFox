<Concept title="What is は?">
は (written as ha, pronounced wa) is the topic marker particle. It marks what the sentence is about.
</Concept>

<Example japanese="わたしはがくせいです。" romaji="Watashi wa gakusei desu." english="I am a student." concept="What is は?" />

<Example japanese="これはほんです。" romaji="Kore wa hon desu." english="This is a book." concept="What is は?" />

<Concept title="は vs が">
は marks the topic (what the speaker is talking about). が marks the grammatical subject.
</Concept>

<Vocab word="わたし" reading="わたし" meaning="I, me" pos="pronoun" />
<Vocab word="がくせい" reading="がくせい" meaning="student" pos="noun" />
<Vocab word="ほん" reading="ほん" meaning="book" pos="noun" />
<Vocab word="です" reading="です" meaning="is/am/are (polite copula)" pos="auxiliary" />

<Sidebar title="Cultural note">
Japanese is a topic-prominent language. The topic は often refers to something already established in context, while が introduces new information.
</Sidebar>
